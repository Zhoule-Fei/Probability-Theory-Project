Weak Law of Large Numbers (WLLN)

  • States that the sample mean converges in probability to the population mean
  • For any ε > 0: P(|X̄ₙ - μ| > ε) → 0 as n → ∞
  • This means the probability that the sample mean differs from the true mean by more than ε approaches zero
  • The histogram shows many trials, and you'll see they cluster more tightly around the true mean with larger samples

Strong Law of Large Numbers (SLLN)

  • States that the sample mean converges almost surely to the population mean
  • P(lim n→∞ X̄ₙ = μ) = 1
  • This is a stronger statement than WLLN - it means each individual sequence converges (not just in probability)
  • The chart shows multiple paths (sequences) and you'll see almost all of them converge to the true mean
  • Each path represents one continuous sequence of increasing sample sizes

Key Difference: WLLN vs SLLN

  • WLLN: Sample mean gets close to true mean with high probability (convergence in probability)
  • SLLN: Sample mean actually reaches true mean as n → ∞ (almost sure convergence)
  • Think of WLLN as: "most experiments will be close" vs SLLN as: "each experiment will eventually converge"
